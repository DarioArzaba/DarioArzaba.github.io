CHAPTER 1 Letter 1: (25/8/91) The main protagonist is named Charlie. Charlie had a classmate named Michael that passed away. The counselor tell Charlie that Michael had “family problems” Charlie’s family consists of 2 older brothers, one brother and one sister. His parents and his aunt Helen. He writes this letters because he’s nervous that he’s about to enter High School. Letter 2: (7/9/91) Charlie doesn’t like High School. His old middle school friend Susan acts different from what she used to. Susan and Michael were best friends so him dying left her depressed. The brother of Charlie plays football and has a Camaro. Letter 3: (11/9/91) Charlie talks about his brother and how he is becoming really good at football. Letter 4: (16/9/91) Talks about his sister and how although she’s pretty she’s also mean to the boys that like her. One of this boys that fell in love write songs and made mix tapes for her, she didn’t like them and Charlie’s dad didn’t like the guy for being so “soft”. But one day he was pushed to far by Charlie’s sister and he hit her really hard. Ironically for Charlie, since then his sister started noticing this guy and going out on dates. Charlie’s father also started to like him. On a Friday Charlie caught them having sex on the sofa, but didn’t say anything. Charlie didn’t feel ok with that relationship but there wasn’t much he could do. Letter 5: (18/9/91) Charlie takes a shop class. One of his classmates in that class is called “Nothing”, but Charlie likes him because he’s funny. Letter 6: (29/9/91) Charlie complains about people saying the same superficial thing every day. He went to the cemetery to visit his dead Aunt Helen; he also starts to realize that his parents are just normal humans. Letter 7: (6/10/91) Charlie encounters “Nothing” at a football game with his sister Sam and they start talking, Charlie discovers that “Nothing” it’s actually named Patrick. Charlie gets a crush with Sam and has a sexual dream with her in it. Letter 8: (14/10/91) Charlie tells Sam about his sexual dream with her, and Patrick advices Charlie to stop thinking about her and look for other girls because Sam is too old for him. Patrick gives Charlie a series of tips on how to get into knowing girls. One day his teacher Bill talks to Charlie and asks him about being all the time thinking about life but not participating in it. Charlie’s sister came home crying because the guy that hit her dump her, and she doesn’t know why. Letter 9: (15/10/91) Charlie’s dad thanks him for telling the truth about the guy hitting her sister. Letter 10: (28/10/91) Charlie tries to participate more in social events so one day he meets up with Sam and Patrick in a football game and then Charlie explained to them that he had never gone to a party, he had just being on one held by his brothers in his house, but they order Charlie to stay in his room. Charlie didn’t had a problem doing this but one drunken couple entered his room looking for a place they could make out, they didn’t mid Charlie was in the room and this guy (named Dave) raped his girlfriend because she didn’t wanted to have sex. Charlie limited to watch the scene and when his sister discovered what happened she got angry with Charlie. After Charlie told this story to Sam and Patrick, Sam explained that it was a difficult situation and there was not much they could do because the girl was still the girlfriend of Dave. The next day Sam and Patrick drove Charlie to his first party. Charlie felt “infinite” and they finally arrive to the party. Bob opened the door and saluted everyone. In the party there was this guy called Fritz Then after Charlie reject a beer bob give him a “Brownie” but it was a pot Brownie and Charlie get stoned. Charlie then wanted to go to the bathroom but he was too high that instead he opened a room and surprised Patrick and Brad (The football player) kissing, they told Charlie to promise never tell anyone about this secret relation. Before Charlie left the party and Patrick came downstairs with Brad he said something really important to Bob about Charlie: “He’s a wallflower”, “You see thing. You keep quiet about them. And you understand.” When the party was over Charlie, Patrick and Sam left in Sam’s pickup to the homecoming dance and even if Charlie didn’t dance he noticed Patrick and Brad didn’t talk because they didn’t want people to know they were in a relationship, and his sister was dancing with the guy that she was prohibited to see. When the dance was over they got into a tunnel where Sam stood up in the back of the pickup to hear the music of the tape and feel the wind. It was really fun. Nobody really knew who had written the poem but Bob said it was a suicide letter. Mary Elizabeth wasn’t Charlie’s Secret Santa; she was of Alice (the other girl with the tattoo). Charlie’s Secret Santa was Patrick after all; he gave him the last piece of his suit, the suit coat. Finally they all started giving each other the last gifts. When it was Charlie’s turn he give Bob a bubble maker, Alice a book by Anne Rice, Mary Elizabeth 40 dollars to print his magazine “Punk Rocky” on color and Sam an old 45 record of “Something” from The Beatles that his Aunt Helen give him before she died. Sam hugged Charlie and whispered “I love you”. Then Sam told Charlie to go to his bedroom so he could have his present, it was an old typewriter with a sheet of paper already attached to it. Sam had written “Write about me sometime” Charlie sit and write in return “I will”. Sam and Charlie smiled. Charlie writed “I love you too” and then Sam looked to Charlie and kissed him. Bill gives a new book for Charlie to read: The catcher in the Rye and he’s also going to be occupied with passing his driver license. He hopes that Sam or Patrick who are in the Great Canon call him. But he gets too anxious because he doesn’t know watch present to buy for his dad, he gets so pressured that he starts having like an asthma attack. Finally he buys a movie so the family can watch together; the problem is that when they arrived from shopping his father and brother are stuck in the traffic with his brother outside the airport because of snow. His birthday dinner was late and this made Charlie a little bit sad, the fact that his family wasn’t united. She was a cheerleader majoring in philosophy, Charlie’s sister started criticizing her and his brother defend her to the point where his brother insulted her saying that at least she didn’t allow him to hit her, this made her cry really hard and Charlie’s father stopped the car and looked into Charlie’s brothers in a way that they knew they shouldn’t have fought. From the anger Charlie’s father didn’t wanted to drive so he ordered Charlie to do it until they arrived at his grandmother’s house. When they arrived at his father-side of the family, his grandmother, his great uncle Phil and his Aunt Rebecca they all watched an old movie, play and had dinner, unfortunately he had a lot of cousins so he had dinner in the kids. Charlie then tells his father-side family story. His grandmother had his first husband that died in Korea with whom she had 2 children. Without a husband and struggling to survive she seeks help in his brother, Charlie’s great uncle Phil. He provided a house and she had to worry only in feeding his babies but as time passed and his children became more time consuming she had to look for another husband and in the desperation she married the wrong guy. Grandmother’s new husband not does only hit his children but he hit her also pretty hard, great uncle Phil noticed this and reunited a group of workers to beat up grandmother’s husband. He died after the beating. The unfortunate thing was that Aunt Rebecca adopted his mother pattern and got married with a lot of guys that beat her just like his father did only to get divorced after. This has lead Charlie’s dad to continuously support Rebecca economically. Charlie reflects on this while lying down on the old bed of his father. Letter 23: (26/12/91) After the reunion ended in Ohio, the family returned home. Then Charlie remembers why every Christmas he’s so sad, he describes his Aunt Helen story: So one Christmas on 1983 his aunt Helen lived with his family because she didn’t had any money left even though she went to buy a second gift for Charlie but because of the thick snow she crashed and died instantly, a policeman came to his house to inform the family, Charlie couldn’t believe it at first but when he finally did, he felt really responsible and guilty of Helen’s death. He passed his license test yesterday without a problem, and when he went to visit her grave he tell her all about his new life and his new friends. Charlie started crying really hard so he also made her the promise of not to cry if it isn’t important things to cry for. With that settled he returned home and distracted himself reading, avoiding crying and having another “panic attack” but no matter how hard he tried he had one, so he thought of going back to the doctor if he couldn’t manage those attacks.
